Australia Rooftop Sensitivity Analysis
====================================

Scope
-----
This report is generated from rooftop_trace_au.txt files in IO_Output/sensitivity_runs.
It compares each sensitivity test against the base run and explains the observed behavior.

What each metric means
----------------------
share: household choice share from the two-option household rooftop choice block.
  In this setup it is effectively the fraction of household demand choosing rooftop over the grid comparator.
rooftop_cap_share: system-wide capacity share = rooftop_capacity / total_power_capacity.
  This is a power-system metric, not only a household rooftop-choice metric.

Why share can be high while rooftop_cap_share is low
----------------------------------------------------
1) Different denominators:
   share compares rooftop vs household grid option in the household block.
   rooftop_cap_share compares rooftop vs ALL generation technologies in the system.
2) Denominator effect after 2030:
   In the base run, rooftop MEWK grows from 49.564200 to 98.954300, but non-rooftop capacity grows faster from 127.070000 to 623.856000.
   Therefore total capacity expands strongly, diluting rooftop's fraction even when rooftop adoption remains strong.
3) Cost signal remains strongly pro-rooftop:
   cost_roof stays very favorable (more negative) from -1221.130000 to -2302.280000, and share rises from 0.846134 to 0.998776.
   So the decline in rooftop_cap_share is not caused by rooftop becoming unattractive.
   It is mainly a composition effect in a rapidly growing system.

Interpretation by test type
---------------------------
discount_low / discount_high:
  These alter discounting and therefore the present value of rooftop savings.
  Lower discount rates should make future savings more valuable and typically improve rooftop economics.
  Higher discount rates do the opposite.
export_0.8 / export_1.2:
  These scale export compensation (value of exported PV electricity).
  Higher export value generally improves rooftop annual benefit and lowers NPC further.
selfcons_minus / selfcons_plus:
  These adjust self-consumption. Higher self-consumption monetizes more generation at retail price,
  improving rooftop economics; lower self-consumption does the opposite.

Main conclusion
---------------
Across all tested sensitivities, rooftop_cap_share peaks around the late 2020s and declines afterward, while share continues to increase toward 1.0.
This pattern is consistent with a plausible model mechanism: rooftop remains attractive for households, but utility-scale and other non-rooftop capacity grows faster in absolute terms, reducing rooftop's share of total system capacity by 2050.
